NOISE ROCK


Noise Rock started much later than its predecessor No Wave as a small but influential offshoot. In a way, the genre is a sort of missing link between Hardcore Punk and Alternative Rock. Inspired by not only Hardcore’s aggression but also its strict and drug-free lifestyle, Noise Rock deliberately turned its back on the excesses of the final Punk Rock movement. Noise Rock artists liked Punk Rock, but they were repulsed by what it had turned into. Idealism and revolution were replaced by nonchalance and chain tweaking. Based on Proto-Punk's experimentation and No Wave’s nihilistic and destructive mentality, Noise Rock used that aversion to fuel songs of high-octane hate. Unsurprisingly, Noise Rock is extremely atonal/dissonant with massive guitar feedback. Riffs are slower than Hardcore Punk, but faster than most Rock and often at low frequency. Singers scream angry lyrics on (or over) the top of their lungs that usually make no sense whatsoever or are highly offensive. The result is a serious contender for the most chaotic of all genres. Though small and unpopular, Noise Rock was a great influence on later music genres, such as Grunge.
