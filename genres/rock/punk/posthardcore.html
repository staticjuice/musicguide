POST-HARDCORE

Post-Hardcore can be either a genre on its own or an umbrella term for any "pure" Hardcore-derivative (i.e. without links to Metal). Just like Post-Punk differs from Punk, so does Post-Hardcore differ from Hardcore as a less bursting, less aggressive continuation of the genre (though still more heavy than other Rock genres). Post-Hardcore implements a longer and more complex arrangement, with tempo changes adding a certain alternating dynamic to Hardcore. Besides screaming or grunting, Post-Hardcore also allows "normal" vocals, usually wailing about depressing or introspective thoughts. Post-Hardcore is dividable into three waves. The first one started in the mid-80s and encloses only some avant-garde bands. The second wave and most important wave was Emocore – a poetic, emotional genre with quiet, slower passages and more artistic lyrics. In the third wave, Screamo became popular and was generally based on the aggressive side of the overarching Emo scene. For many groups, lyrics often boasted violent or bloody imagery alongside subject matter dealing with overwhelming angst and relationships.
