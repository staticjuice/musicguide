POST-HARDCORE


Post-Hardcore can be either a genre on its own or an umbrella term for any “pure” Hardcore-derivative (i.e. without links to Metal). Just like Post-Punk differs from Punk, so does Post-Hardcore differ from Hardcore as a less bursting, less aggressive continuation of the genre (though still more heavy than other Rock genres). Post-Hardcore implements a longer and more complex arrangement, with tempo changes adding a certain alternating dynamic to Hardcore. Besides screaming or grunting, Post-Hardcore also allows “normal” vocals, usually wailing about depressing or introspective thoughts. Post-Hardcore is dividable into three waves. The first one started in the mid eighties and encloses only a number of avant-garde bands. In the second wave, Post-Punk along with its Dub and Fusion influences further molded Post-Hardcore into a versatile adventurer. The third and most important wave was Emocore – a poetic, emotional genre with quiet, slower passages and more artistic lyrics. Emocore tries to break the ties with Original Hardcore, which was deemed too conservative and uniform.
