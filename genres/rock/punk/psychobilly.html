PSYCHOBILLY

1981

Psychobilly is a weird and unusual fusion genre that never received much attention, although it has a recognisable sound and substantial subculture. In the late 70s, Punk artists were drawn towards Rockabilly. The mixture of these genres processed a remarkable outcome – creepy Rock & Roll with haunting echos, spooky sound effects and rough chainsaw riffs. It is downright crazy music like the band had lost their brain and became zombified, hence the word Psychobilly. Vocals are also often sung in a stuttering manner, reminiscent of Rockabilly. The related Horror Punk is a genre defined by a large amount of nostalgia for 50s imagery, fashion, themes and especially Horror films. Horror Punk shares a great deal of overlapping with Psychobilly as both genres like to indulge in a subculture of B-flicks and cult movies. Horror Punk and Psychobilly are among the few subgenres of Punk that don't take themselves seriously (on the contrary), which explains a great deal of their popularity. Shortly after its birth, Psychobilly directly leads to the second wave of Garage Rock (a minor revival movement).
