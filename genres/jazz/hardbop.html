HARD BOP


Hard Bop is a more modern evolution of Bebop, but not particularly “harder”, despite the term. The music is smoothened out and more emphasis is being put on the rhythm. The “Cool School” of the west coast had also provoked a certain disdain by Bebop players, with its exaggerated rationalism and lack of pathos. Hard Bop formed the emotional answer but it in a very basic and gentle manner and not chaotic at all. Alongside these influences, the arrival of longplay records (at 33rpm) allowed for longer songs and longer solos. Numerous alternating solos and chords (of different instruments, sometimes even bass and drums) are being played in a call and response manner. Hard Bop is played by small ensembles and is often described as having a more “bodied” or “textured” sound. Since its inception, Hard Bop has been the dominant genre in Jazz. When Jazz is performed in Jazz bars or performances, it is highly likely to be Hard Bop. There are two Hard Bop subgenres – Funky Jazz (simple, with bluesy sound) and Mainstream Hard Bop (more complex, closer related to Bebop).
