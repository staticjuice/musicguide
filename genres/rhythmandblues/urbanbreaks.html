URBAN BREAKS


Where the first wave of Urban was still linked to original forms of R&B, a second wave broke all those ties and became a monster dance floor phenomenon. Here we have a soft and smooth type of Hip Hop/R&B music but with big hooks and heavy synth basslines, as well as strong, loud and heavily autotuned choruses alternated by rap verses. Instead of focusing on gigs and albums like its predecessor, Urban Breaks focuses more on singles, remixes and DJ-friendly music. The music genre became immensely popular, but not just for its catchy sound. Major record labels and clever producers marketed Urban Breaks to the youth and simply labeled it “R&B”, leading to a great deal of ignorance about the term to this day. It is often considered the most commercial genre of all time, and therefore also one of the most despised, especially by true Hip Hop and R&B artists who feel their genres have been violated. But Urban Breaks is also adored, especially by a young female crowd, to which the slow but hooky breakbeat rhythm is addictive and encourages sexual dancing, which in turn attracts a large male audience.
