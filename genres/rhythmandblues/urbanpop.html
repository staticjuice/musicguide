URBAN POP

1998

What is referred to as Urban Pop is so omnipresent that it's often identified with the general term R&B. Here we have a soft and smooth type of Hip Hop and R&B music with big hooks and strong, loud and heavily autotuned choruses alternated by rap verses. The music genre became immensely popular, but not just for its catchy sound. Major record labels and clever producers marketed this sound to the youth and simply labelled it "R&B", leading to a great deal of ignorance about the term to this day. It is often considered the most commercial genre of all time, and therefore also one of the most despised, especially by Hip Hop and R&B artists who feel their genres have been violated. But Urban Pop is also adored, especially by a young female crowd, to which the slow but hooky breakbeat rhythm is addictive and encourages sexual dancing, which in turn attracts a largely male audience. Although the genre might be considered formulaic Pop music, this simply proves that it succeeds in finding that perfect formula with Hip Hop, Pop, and R&B ingredients.
