LIQUID FUNK


Who would have thought that a small experiment of compiling a bunch of Jazzstep artists with a different, friendlier and more uptempo sound could turn out into Drum & Bass gaining massive mainstream appeal? While the original sound harboured more of a jazzy vibe, later tracks started incorporating Disco and House influences. Even more than Jazzstep, production is extremely polished, resulting in a delicate, colourful, upbeat yet emotional sound. Whereas any other Drum & Bass genre is music for the night, Liquid Funk is music for the day – to be played at festivals or simply at home. Over the years, Liquid Funk evolves from Jazzstep with some Funk elements to a faster and richer or layered sound, and finally to a heavily vocalized and exuberant one (very uncommon in the world of D&B). This last style has gained quite a momentum after 2010, due to ever popular catchy vocals and collaboration with other artists, bridging the gap between Drum & Bass and Pop music. A vibrant Liquid club scene is alive throughout the world, where people prefer the optimistic or sentimental sound over nihilistic, dark, industrial Rave beats.
