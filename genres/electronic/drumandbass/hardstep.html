HARDSTEP


By the mid-90s, Rave was completely dead. A new subculture had learned from its mistakes. No more huge pop-up parties, but a pirate radio network broadcasting revolting Drum & Bass instead. This hardcore Drum & Bass (Hardstep) was simple, purist music but with a very heavy sub-bassline, fast, 2-step drum patterns and lots of influences from and references to Hip Hop, Ragga and Dub. This is the mid-90s precursor to Techstep, when the familiar 2-step drum pattern (boom cha, boom-cha) started infiltrating all areas of the genre, which coincided with the replacing of the word Jungle with Drum & Bass. Hardstep is essentially a harder style of Old Skool Drum & Bass which uses gritty basslines and heavy yet simple electronic melodies. It had more or less the same mentality as Conscious Rap & an underground army with back to the roots (black culture) music. Hardstepâ€™s history however, was rather small and short-lived. It had speed and percussion, but lacked melody and atmosphere. Notable pioneers of the genre include Dillinja, DJ Krust, Mampi Swift, Dieselboy, Current Value, Tre Technics and MachineCode.
