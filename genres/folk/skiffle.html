SKIFFLE


Skiffle is one of the oldest popular music genres. Emerging in the 20s in the United States, early Skiffle can be seen as a Folk influenced form of proto-Country. Skiffle was music for the poorest of people, who created a true jack-of-all-trades musical melting pot with whatever instrument they could lay their hands on, as long as it was cheap – fiddle, banjo, kazoo and household items such as washboards. The biggest challenge was an inexpensive alternative for bass, which they found in large jugs. Jug Bands even had to throw pop-up house parties to pay the rent and their instruments, which were called “Skiffles”. In the early 50s a Skiffle Revival takes place in the United Kingdom, parallel to the rise of early R&B and Rock & Roll. Because Britain lacked the history of Country music, almost all musicians struggled with creating Rock & Roll music. In response to American R&R, poor British students responded with a type of Folk-Blues-Rock, that was very loud, fast and noisy. They used the same instruments as older American Jug Bands alongside cheap acoustic guitars. But by the late 50s, Rock & Roll became so big that Skiffle quickly decayed.
