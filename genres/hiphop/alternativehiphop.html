ALTERNATIVE HIP HOP

Out of Jazz Rap, a strong and endless Hip Hop movement emerged. Next to the aggressiveness of Gangsta Rap and vulgarity of Miami Bass, the Nu Skool offered a third alternative for Hip Hop fans who wanted more than these transparent provocateurs. As a parallel underground stream, the Nu Skool experimented more with techniques and creating own sounds, earning the name Progressive or Alternative hip Hop. Instead of nicely matching the rhyme to a predetermined pattern, Nu Skool rappers became lyrical wordsmiths, striking their verbal avalanches at any unguarded point of the beat. Alternative Hip Hop is sometimes divided into three subgenres, based on their lyrical content – Conscious Hip Hop, Instrumental Hip Hop and Political Hip Hop. Even more than Jazz Rap, Conscious Hip Hop stresses the native roots of Africa, often identifying with radical African tribes or militant black Muslims. Instrumental Hip Hop focuses on special techniques and unexpected rhythms where content is less important than form. Political Hip Hop, on the other hand, involves a sharp and relentless critique on government and corporate society.
