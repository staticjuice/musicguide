GANGSTA RAP


Gangsta Rap was not only anti-state but also anti-rappers, meaning other posses or fake rappers. The proudness of succeeding in genuine criminal entrepreneurship while dealing with emotional struggle and dissing other rappers, was the stereotypical image of the gangsta rapper. This attitude gained a huge appeal across the country. These rappers took more samples from Disco and Soul (piano and strings) rather than Funk (bass guitars, synth), but even Rock was possible. Gangsta rappers tried to attain less of a ghetto thug image and a lot more of a well-dressed, rich, mobster image. This is where the original meaning of “bling-bling” got lost and caught up in the mix of richness and status symbols such as sports cars, swimming pools and mansions. One must take note that this is actually the second wave of Gangsta Rap. The first one emerged during Rap’s Golden Age and never attained the huge record sales the successive wave had. Eventually Gangsta Rap evolves more and more into a mainstream radio format and loses all hardcore edges to the point where separation from Urban Breaks becomes impossible.
