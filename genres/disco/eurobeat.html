EUROBEAT

1986

Eurobeat is a high-energy genre of Disco that originated in Europe during the late 80s. It evolved from Italo Disco and Hi-NRG, characterised by its fast tempo, typically ranging from 140 to 160 BPM, and a distinctive, pulsating rhythm. The genre is known for its catchy melodies, powerful synths, and upbeat, energetic vibe, often featuring prominent basslines, bright, staccato melodies, and enthusiastic vocals. Eurobeat gained a significant following in Japan, where it became closely associated with the parapara dance culture and anime which featured numerous Eurobeat tracks in its soundtracks. This Japanese influence helped propel the genre to international recognition. Distinctive elements of Eurobeat include rapid-fire drum patterns, euphoric chord progressions, and repetitive yet infectious hooks. Lyrics often revolve around themes of love, dancing, and high energy, adding to the genre's feel-good, adrenaline-pumping atmosphere. Eurobeat continues to have a dedicated fanbase, particularly in Japan, where it remains a staple in dance clubs and anime culture.
