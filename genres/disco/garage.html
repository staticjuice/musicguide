GARAGE

1979

Garage music is a genre of Dance music that originated in the late 70s and early 80s in New York City. It predated and influenced the development of House music. The genre is named after the Paradise Garage, a famed nightclub where DJ Larry Levan played a pivotal role in shaping its sound. Garage music has soulful vocals, smooth basslines, and lush, melodic instrumentation. It typically features a slower tempo compared to House music, focusing more on groove and emotional expression rather than the relentless, driving beats of later Dance genres. The music often incorporates elements of Disco, R&B, and Funk, with a strong emphasis on musicality and sophisticated arrangements. Key elements of Garage music include rich, layered production with live instruments such as keyboards, bass, and drums, vocals that are often deeply expressive, and a focus on creating a warm, inviting atmosphere conducive to dancing and socialising. Garage music laid the groundwork for the development of House music and later influenced various other genres within the Dance music spectrum.
