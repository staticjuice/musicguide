PRAISE BREAK


Praise Break isn't so much a subgenre of Gospel music than it is a style of praise and worship often found in many African-American charismatic churches. From a musical perspective, Praise Break is essentially Modern Gospel in overdrive. Being a lot faster, more joyful and musically complex than Modern Gospel, Praise Break encourages members of the congregation to forget all their troubles and just express their emotions in the form of dancing, not worrying about the perception of others around them. Many musicians of this style of music as well as Modern Gospel are typically expert musicians, having being brought up in the church and being actively involved in the music since a very young age. Praise Break also tends to employ a very fast and complex style of drumming known as the gospel chop – one of the most technical drumming styles ever developed. But it isn't just the drums that are highly technical – the keyboard and other instruments are too. Praise Break ultimately highlights just how immortal Gospel is as a genre, with the style continuing to flourish to this day.
