URBAN COUNTRY

1980

The so-called second wave of Country Rock actually shifted Country further away from its original roots. Instead of becoming more hardcore, it turned into a slick and glossy archetype. It was barely Rock at all, it was more Country Pop. The popularity of "Urban Cowboy", a film from 1978 starring John Travolta, got the style going and also lended its name to the genre. With poster boys and girls singing catchy, emotional songs without much tempo or volume change, they created sentimental soundtrack music that seemed ripped right off the next big romance or teenage blockbuster. Electric guitars and pianos were used to give Country a more modern feel and Pop Rock and Soft Rock influences were used to appeal to an even bigger audience. The hip image of newer country dance clubs, like in Urban Cowboy, became an exploited illusion to sell this music en masse. The commercial (or even greedy) mindset of Urban Cowboy evoked a strong backlash by a new wave of alternative Country musicians who explored opposite directions with Americana.
