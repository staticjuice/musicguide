SEAPUNK

2011

Seapunk is a subculture that originated on Tumblr in the early 2010s. It is often associated with an aquatic-themed style of fashion, 3D net art, iconography and allusions to popular culture of the 90s. The advent of Seapunk also spawned its own electronic music microgenre, featuring elements of Hip Hop, Pop and 90s R&B music, gaining limited popularity as it spread through the internet, although it was said to have developed its own club scene. Seapunk incorporates bits of 90s House, the past 15 years of Pop and R&B and the latest in Trap – all overlaid with a twinkly, narcotic energy that recalls New Age music and chopped and screwed Hip Hop mixtapes in roughly equal measure. Seapunks often dye their hair, and sometimes facial hair, with varying shades of turquoise, lilac and sea blue. Images featuring neon flashing colours and rotating geometric shapes floating above oceans of brilliant blue or green water flood the pages tagged with a #seapunk hashtag on Tumblr. The aforementioned imagery has given rise to other internet-based subgenres consisting of similar themes, such as Slimepunk and Icepunk.
