LO-FI

2013

An infinite loop of a tired anime girl, sitting in a picturesque Miyazaki bedroom, writing revisions into her notebook, or reading a newspaper with a hot cup of coffee, or simply staring out of a lonely window on a rainy day. A very particular, very millennial breed of simmering Downtempo Hip Hop pours through your laptop speakers – muffled drum machine pitter, a few lazy nocturnal synth beams, perhaps an entranced, elliptical vocal sample sourced from self-help tapes, ancient cartoons or old-school Nintendo 64 games. These ingredients are simple and remarkably consistent, and they add up into a new incarnation of internet radio called "Lo-Fi Hip Hop," or "Chillhop," or more pointedly, "Lo-Fi Hip Hop Anime Chill Beats To Study And Relax To". Endless, non-perishable YouTube streams that run 24/7, delivering the chillest, most amicable vibes to a legion of traumatised university students – like the perfect holistic alternative when the Xanax just isn't cutting it anymore. Sometimes, all you need to do is trust the taste of an enterprising kid with a YouTube channel, and your next study session is sorted.
