HYPERPOP

2014

Hyperpop emerged in the mid to late 2010s and is characterised by its eclectic and exaggerated approach to Pop music. It blends elements of Pop, Electronic, Hip Hop, and Experimental music, often featuring high-pitched vocals, distorted beats, and glossy production. Hyperpop artists push the boundaries of Pop music, embracing digital manipulation and avant-garde techniques to create a futuristic sound. Key artists in the genre include SOPHIE, 100 gecs, Charli XCX, and A.G. Cook, who is also known for founding the influential PC Music label. These artists often explore themes of identity, technology, and consumerism, frequently subverting more mainstream Pop conventions. The genre is also noted for its inclusivity and celebration of LGBTQ+ identities, with many Hyperpop artists being part of the queer community. Hyperpop's visual aesthetics are equally distinctive, often featuring vibrant, surreal, and cyber-inspired imagery. This genre challenges listeners with its unconventional sounds and provides a fresh, boundary-pushing take on contemporary music.
