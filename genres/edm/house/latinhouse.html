LATIN HOUSE

1990

Shortly after Funky House was developed, House producers realised that the upbeat and lively sounds of Latin music worked beautifully when combined with House music. Originating in urban centres like Miami, Chicago, and New York, it was heavily influenced by the rich musical heritage of Latino communities in these areas. The genre's signature sound is characterised by the fusion of traditional Latin instruments, such as congas, bongos, and timbales, with the electronic beats and basslines of House music. This combination creates a dynamic, danceable groove that is both familiar and innovative. Vocals often feature in Spanish or Portuguese, adding a distinct cultural flavour. The syncopated rhythms and use of percussive elements create a vibrant, infectious energy that sets Latin House apart from other House subgenres. Even today, Latin House still remains a prominent style in the Funky House scene, and will likely remain popular in decades to come, just like the endless fun of parties and Carnival in the streets and beaches of South America.
