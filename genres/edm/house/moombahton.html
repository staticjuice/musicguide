In a time of music genre saturation, new genres are often invented by the unexpected fusion of two remote, distinctive genres. Moombahton is such an example – Dutch House meets Reggaeton. Thus Moombahton is actually a form of House music, but slowed down and filled with Reggaeton sounds and rhythms, until it is barely recognisable as such. The name “Moombah” comes from the first conceived track. Moombahton is syncopated beat-heavy club music, with a natural summer feeling. Although the tempo is incredibly low in the world of EDM, the party doesn't slow down when Moombahton is around. Moombahton is loved for combining heavy drops, hooks and beats with shrill synths, but without taking itself seriously. Songs are often very short, indicating that they are mostly used for a quick taste of the drop and main synth melody. This subgenre is a good example of how House, Trap, and Dubstep (the "heavy drop"-genres) have become so intertwined that there is actually very few music that belongs strictly to one genre – the majority falls in between.
