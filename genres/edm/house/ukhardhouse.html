UK HARD HOUSE

1995

UK Hard House is the ultimate bumper car music – the soundtrack of the fairground – flashing, pounding, building up and breaking loose. UK Hard House is characterized by hard bass stabs (with its signature "donk" sound), silly samples, hoovers, and a fast tempo of 140 to 150 BPM. Breakdowns are intensified, either by silence or drum rolls, and prelude harsh climaxes. Vocals, if present, are usually minimal and repetitive, adding to the hypnotic and driving nature of the tracks. Because the American Press uses Hard House as an umbrella label for any type of Hard Dance, the specific term "UK Hard House" is being reserved for this genre (a poor choice, considering the fact that Hard House was more popular in Germany, the Netherlands, and Belgium. In more recent years, UK Hard House saw a new revival where it reinvented itself in Russia where its known as Hardbass. The genre remains celebrated for its relentless energy and ability to create an electrifying atmosphere on the dance floor. UK Hard House continues to be loved for its uncompromising intensity and the unyielding drive to make people dance.
