Bass House is a dynamic genre of House that merges the deep, heavy basslines of UK Garage and Dubstep with the rhythmic, four-to-the-floor beat of House music. Emerging in the mid-2010s, it’s known for its high-energy drops, infectious grooves, and aggressive yet danceable vibe. Producers often incorporate elements of Grime, Trap, and Hip Hop, resulting in a sound that's both gritty and polished. Key artists like Jauz, Joyryde, and AC Slater have propelled the genre forward, infusing their tracks with pulsating sub-bass, intricate sound design, and catchy vocal hooks. Bass House tracks typically feature distorted basslines, punchy drums, and shuffled hi-hats, creating a driving, club-ready sound that's perfect for festivals and underground raves alike. The genre's versatility allows for a broad range of experimentation, from dark and intense to bright and funky. Given the popularity of Dubstep and other Bass music styles, it seemed inevitable that people would eventually package that sound into a 128 BPM, four-to-the-floor House beat.
