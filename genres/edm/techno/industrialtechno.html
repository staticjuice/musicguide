INDUSTRIAL TECHNO

1991

Detroit Techno only had very limited popularity in the States. American DJs were forced to turn their eye on Europe and found the perfect breeding ground for Techno in Germany. Germans were thirsty for hard, industrial, uncompromising beats, and under the influence of the German New Wave, New Beat, and EBM, Techno changes into a harder and faster version of itself but particularly more abrasive. This next wave of Techno is thereby an adaptation for a European audience by American producers. Although early records were more a part of Detroit Techno, the later works form a strong contrast with it, due to clearly a harder and rugged sound. Belgium embraces American Techno producers as well, where some start to develop a slightly more euphoric sound towards Rave. Around 1994, German Techno becomes even harder and faster, with maximum-intensity kick drums and a higher BPM. Industrial Techno exhibits a long evolution of Detroit Techno once it sets foot on European soil – a genre that clearly is hard to constrain, but rather a lineage that continuously strengthens and accelerates.
