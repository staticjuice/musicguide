HARD TECHNO

1993

Hard Techno is where Classic Techno meets the post-Rave cultural movement. Many producers were eager to try out the faster BPM and hyperactive sound effects of Hardcore in a slightly more subtle, rhythmically looping approach. The genre really burst open when British Rave producers and sound system crews were forced to flee the country after the initiation of the Criminal Justice Act in 1994. The result was Rave bent towards Techno – harsh, extremely fast and pounding. In a wild and rapidly spreading scene across the European continent, parties and DJs popped up out of nowhere. Suddenly anyone and everyone could make Dance music, and Techno was no longer an American elite underground thing. In Europe, it became a full-scale, DIY, liberating pop culture. The corresponding cultural movement is very similar to Rave but on a much smaller scale – sound systems and Techno communities join up at free pop-up parties, usually at illegal places. Despite trouble with authorities, it helped spread Dance awareness and spearheaded official funding for all EDM festivals and parties.
