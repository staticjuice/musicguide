RIDDIM

2014

Riddim, a subgenre of Dubstep, emerged in the mid-2010s and is recognised for its minimalist approach, repetitive patterns, and intense focus on bass. The name "Riddim" comes from the Jamaican Patois word for "rhythm," reflecting its emphasis on creating a hypnotic, rhythm-driven experience. The sound of Riddim is defined by deep, wobbling basslines that dominate the track, delivering a pulsating energy that drives the music forward. The compositions are typically simple and repetitive, emphasising rhythmic and textural variations rather than complex melodies. Syncopated rhythms and swung beats give Riddim its distinctive groove, creating an immersive listening experience. Sound design plays a crucial role in Riddim, with extensive use of modulation, filters, and effects shaping the bass and other elements to produce a gritty, wobbly texture. Melodies and vocal samples are often sparse, allowing the bass and rhythm to take centre stage. Riddim has carved out a significant niche within the Dubstep community, appreciated for its raw energy and deep, bass-heavy grooves.
