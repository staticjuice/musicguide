MIDTEMPO BASS

2016

Midtempo Bass emerged in the late 2010s, characterised by its dark, heavy sound and slower tempos, typically ranging from 100 to 110 BPM. This genre blends elements of Bass music, Electro, and Industrial, creating a gritty and powerful auditory experience. Distinctive for its thick, distorted basslines and aggressive rhythms, Midtempo Bass often incorporates ominous atmospheres and intricate sound design. The slower tempo allows for a more deliberate, head-nodding groove compared to faster-paced EDM genres. Tracks frequently feature sharp, mechanical synths, eerie pads, and cinematic effects that contribute to a dystopian, industrial feel. Rezz’s hypnotic, dark soundscapes and 1788-L’s glitchy, futuristic tracks exemplify the genre's unique blend of intensity and atmosphere. Their music often incorporates elements of horror and science fiction, enhancing the genre's otherworldly and immersive quality. Midtempo Bass has gained a dedicated following for its ability to fuse heavy bass with a slower, more controlled tempo, offering a fresh and intense listening experience.
