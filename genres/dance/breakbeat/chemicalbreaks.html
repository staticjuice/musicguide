CHEMICAL BREAKS

1993

Chemical Breaks, sometimes overlapping with Big Beat, is a form of Breakbeat that was popular in Britain during the 90s. It's typcially used to describe the music of artists such as The Chemical Brothers, The Crystal Method and The Prodigy. Chemical Breaks has tempos around 120-140 BPM, features heavy and often distorted synthetic-sounding beats, TB-303 synth lines resembling Acid House and heavy loops from 70s Funk, Soul and Jazz. They are often driven by Punk vocalists and intense, distorted synth basslines. Chemical Breaks usually follows conventional House, Techno and Pop structure. The songs include many effects, such as crescendos, builds and drops, extended drum rolls and spoken word samples. Some songs would even add non-conventional instruments such as Middle Eastern strings and sitars. Fatboy Slim used heavily compressed, loud breakbeats in his songs, driving the energy up and adding Rock and Industrial aesthetics to the music, eventually evolving into true Big Beat. This associates it with Jungle and Drum & Bass, the only difference being a slower tempo.
