TECHSTEP

1995

Toward the end of the 90s, Hardstep gets pushed aside by the successor of Darkstep and gives birth to the rise of Techstep. This technical Drum & Bass, influenced by Industrial Techno, barely had anything in common with the funky, sophisticated Detroit sound. Filled with heavily manipulated Reese-basslines, sci-fi references and so-called "butcher beats", this should've been called Industrial Drum & Bass – loud, uncompromising, grotesque, nihilistic factory noise. Techstep almost immediately saw the potential of heavy Drum & Bass thanks to Hardstep, and took it up another notch. Full of sound effect experimentation, it was an antimusical sadomasochistic soundtrack, just like early Industrial but painted for a gritty post-Rave selfish world, instead of a conceptual movement. Though Techstep is stylistically more linked to its predecessor Darkstep, its joining with Hardstep illustrates Drum & Bass' rapid transformation in the mid-90s and what that expresses & a requiem for a lost Rave dream, ruptured into an aural nightmare, like Heavy Metal after the end of the flower power age.
