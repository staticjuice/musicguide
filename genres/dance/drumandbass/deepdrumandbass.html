DEEP D&B

2005

Just as Liquid Funk is the combination of Disco and Drum & Bass and Neurofunk is Industrial and Drum & Bass, Deep D&B is Tech House mixed with Drum & Bass. And not just any Tech House, but the Minimal variety that dominated German clubs for an entire decade in the 2000s. Its prominant use of deep basslines and rolling beats makes this subgenre a staple at many Drum & Bass parties. The bassline keeps on rolling like ocean waves. More importantly, "rolling" is slang for being under the influence of hard party drugs, in particular ecstasy. Rollers were the main style of music played at parties flying under the "Drum & Bass" flag for a long time, and this concept predates other D&B subgenres such as Jump Up and Neurofunk by at least a decade. Those who attempted to make Deep D&B were swept up in the Dubstep and Drumstep craze as soon as it was temporally prudent to do so, and Deep D&B remains a small side genre that might still exist but probably doesn't. But Deep D&B fills a niche in the Drum & Bass scene by being dark and brooding, yet minimal and chilled out at the same time.
