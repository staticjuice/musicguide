JUMP UP

Drum & Bass transformed in an avalanche of subgenres in a minimal time-span. These genres were significantly more complex and sophisticated than before. The main reason for this was the compactness and affordability of hard disk recorders, replacing traditional multi-track (reel-to-reel) recorders and which also allowed non-linear editing. Because of Drum & Bass' historical connection with marijuana, an unexpected problem arose with these complex subgenres. The behaviour of stationary nodding to the beat became commonplace and alarmed a number of producers. They wanted the crowd to party, which lead to a genre specifically aimed at doing so & Jump Up. Definitely more heavy and bass-focused than early Drum & Bass but still less dark than Hardstep and Techstep, Jump Up seeks to combine the most popular elements of Jungle, for which it sometimes also gets despised – simple, catchy melodies, Ragga basslines and speedy drum loops. Lately, Jump Up has been revived with a drier, more minimal second wave, called Nu Jump Up, being a sort of fusion between Jump Up and Dubstep.
