DARKSTEP

1992

Evolving directly from the Breakbeat Hardcore part of the early 90s Rave scene, the trinity of pioneering Jungle genres (Jungle, Drum & Bass and Darkcore) almost emerged simultaneously and it's still very hard to say which influenced which. While D&B and Jungle sounded quite similar, Darkstep was something different. It was literally the dark side of Jungle & ecstasy addicts (as were many ravers) being confronted with the side-effects of intensive drug abuse. Darkstep was the after-XTC-soundtrack & a nightmare of paranoia, hallucinations and depression, being intensified by government suppression of Rave parties. Disturbing sounds, awkward time-stretching techniques and Horror film samples combined with an ultrafast tempo of 180 BPM, provided adequate ammunition to combat the "stupid" Rave scene. Darkstep is recognisable by undoubtedly being the darkest of all Drum & Bass subgenres. Darkstep and Techstep evolve in the 21st century into Dark Drum & Bass aka Darkside – a more modern and eclectic approach that can also serve as an umbrella term for Hardstep, Techstep, Darkcore, Darkstep and Neurofunk.
