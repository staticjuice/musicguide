BREAKBEAT HARDCORE

1990

Breakbeat Hardcore is an important Dance genre with a fairly complex evolution. Early Hardcore producers started to make accelerated, silly Dance music that became quite popular within Rave culture. Breakbeat Hardcore has fast breakbeats, half-speed basslines, and is known for its amateur, DIY sound with simplistic artwork and an arsenal of illegal samples. A huge underground pirate movement took matters into their own hands, churning out record after record. Immediately afterwards, Breakbeat Hardcore became influenced by Ragga (resulting in Proto Drum & Bass). Fueled by massive XTC consumption, all these genres resulted in a much more expansive, second wave of Rave. This wave differs from the first one due to a happier and more uptempo sound. All the dark and heavy influences were thrown overboard and speedy piano melodies onto looping breakbeats were introduced instead. The lyrics often dealt either about extreme happiness or nightlife and dancing. For its fans, all these elements intertwine into pure ecstatic, celestial music as if one had found the gates to heaven.
