SPEEDCORE

Welcome to the world of Extreme Gabber. The grip Gabber had on the world of Hardcore was so strong that both genre names are used synonymously, and fragmentation into subgenres was bound to occur. Speedcore is so fast that there are barely any other adjectives to describe it. Any description of sound gets lost in the ultra-acceleration of Gabber beats. Because Speedcore is all about speed, the only way to introduce variation is going even faster – eventually this got faster and faster resulting in Splittercore (over 700 bpm) and finally Extratone (1000 bpm+). In this final frontier, beats succeed each other so rapidly that a single tone is all that remains. In France, another subgenre known as Frenchcore gains popularity with a far more distorted, grittier sound and a "moderate" tempo (up to 200 bpm). Eventually these styles became independent Hardcore schools that would gain their own club rooms or party stages.
