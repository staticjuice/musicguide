DETROIT TECHNO

Although officially originated from House, Techno already had a slumbering pre-existence before that. In the depressing, cold motor city of Detroit, where high unemployment rates gave little reason to celebrate, a select few composers were working with analog sequencers on another type of Dance music. Something not meant for upper class euphoric celebration, but as a soundtrack for the lower class of this city of abandoned factories and machine-like rhythm. Based on the blueprint of Funk-influenced Electro, Techno evolved, borrowing the pounding beat from Chicago House into a fast rhythm. Detroit Techno was first and foremost the brainchild of the three legendary Godfathers of Techno, known as the Belleville Three – Juan Atkins (the Originator), Kevin Saunderson (the Elevator) and Derrick May (the Innovator). The Belleville Three were already experimenting with electronic music in 1982, building further on the Electro legacy of Detroit.
