TROPICAL HOUSE

Another genre to emerge from the musical characteristics of Deep House is Tropical House. Originating in the mid-2010s from artists such as Thomas Jack, Kygo and Matoma, Tropical House initially started off small but ended up becoming a huge phenomenon, eventually finding its way into the top music charts worldwide and heavily influencing the course of Pop music in general. Tropical House possesses typical House music characteristics, including synthesizer instrumentation and a 4/4 kick drum pattern. However, Tropical House differentiates itself from Deep House by having a more uplifting and relaxing sound. When the style first emerged, the tempo of Tropical House songs was considerably slower than other forms of House music, even a little slower than Deep House (110-115 BPM). Tropical House tends to utilize a minimal amount of synth and Electro elements, instead preferring to include tropical instruments such as steel drums, marimbas and pan flutes. As the genre gained popularity, Tropical House began to use traditional House tempos and overall became much more upbeat and dance-friendly compared to its origins.
