SOFT ROCK


Some Arena Rock bands took a lighter approach (no guitar solos and less bass) and created Soft Rock. What started as an alternative countermovement became so commercial that eventually it was pure Pop music. The outcome and legacy of Arena Rock was a large and soft genre, yet not targeted at teens (compared to other Pop), but specifically for adults – hence the synonym Adult Contemporary. The name Adult Contemporary did not start out as a genre but as a billboard category for music, officially installed in 1979. Adult Contemporary replaced the Easy-Listening category, and as such it is rather lightweight music for more mature people who like their music relaxing and politically correct. Unsurprisingly, there is quite some overlapping with similar genres such as Country Pop, Smooth Jazz, New Age and Singer-Songwriter. In the eighties, an offshoot known as Sophisti-Pop emerged, which uses more synth than regular Soft Rock, though subdivision within Soft Rock is a bit hair splitting. Soft Rock is still being broadcasted on specialized radio channels throughout the world, as these golden classics seem to have a timeless appeal.
