SYNTHPOP


In the 80s, Synthpop reigns supreme – electronic New Wave that became highly popular due to the combination of a recognizable Pop structure and new and exciting synthesizer sounds. The release of the Yamaha DX7 in 1983 as the essential synthesizer dramatically increases the production of Synthpop music. Guitars are barely noticeable, if they are present at all or not replaced by keytars. The difference between original New Wave and mid-80s Synthpop is quite large. It’s less aggressive and more melodic, with less Rock and more Pop. It’s sleek and sterile, contagious and danceable, accessible and fun. Another difference is the increased emphasis on fashion, style, glamour and make-up. In this momentum a temporary movement also existed, known as the New Romantics – a hybrid of Synthpop and New Wave. Synthpop can be a misleading term, as the genre is probably the least Pop-like of all Pop genres in terms of mentality. Many Synthpop artists crave experimentalism and conceptual music, building further on the legacy of Post-Punk. Synthpop was also one of the few music genres heavily featured on early MTV.
