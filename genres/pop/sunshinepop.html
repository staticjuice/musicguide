SUNSHINE POP

Sunshine Pop is a lightly-produced subgenre of Pop music originating in Southern California in the mid- to late-60s, beginning as an outgrowth of the earlier Folk Rock movement. Rooted in Easy Listening, advertising jingles and the growing drug culture, the music is characterized by lush vocals and light arrangements, combining nostalgic or anxious moods with "an appreciation for the beauty of the world". Sunshine Pop enjoyed mainstream success in the latter half of the decade, with many of its top 40 hits peaking in the spring and summer of 1967, especially just before the Summer of Love. Most of the acts were lesser-known bands named after fruits, colours or cosmic concepts who imitated more popular groups like The Beach Boys and The Mamas & The Papas. In some ways the genre is similar to Baroque Pop music through being elaborate and melancholic, though it also crossed into Folk Rock and Brill Building styles. Sunshine Pop may be seen as a form of escapism from the turmoil of the times, mixed with a sense of anxiety that the good ol' days were gone for good.
