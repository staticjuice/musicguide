ALTERNATIVE POP

2009

Alternative Pop, or Alt-Pop, is a genre of Pop music with broad commercial appeal created by artists outside the mainstream. It is seen as more original and eclectic compared to other styles of Pop, often featuring darker and more introspective themes like insecurity, regret, drugs, and anxiety. Alt-Pop resonates more authentically with teenage experiences, offering a personalised take on mainstream music. During the 80s, "left-of-centre Pop" in the Alternative scene didn't achieve mainstream success, but by the early 2000s, a new wave of Alt-Pop artists began to emerge, setting the stage for future musicians. The late 2000s saw the rise of artists with strong artistic conviction. By the early 2010s, a cinematic and melancholic style of Alt-Pop gained a devoted following, achieving global success and winning awards. Throughout the 2010s, Pop music increasingly took cues from Alt-Pop, integrating its sad and moody tones into mainstream music. By 2022, alternative pop had firmly ascended into the mainstream, marked by its darker and more introspective style.
