EURODISCO


It was only a matter of time before the New Wave movement would spin out of control. At the far end of the spectrum was Hi-NRG – a type of Disco/Synthpop where there was absolutely no trace of Rock left to find. This was pure Pop music – slickly produced Synthpop melodies driven by the pulsing rhythm of drum machines. Over-the-top, decadent and ecstatic Disco indulged in a world of fashion, discotheques and upper class style and heavily associated with homosexual parties. Eurodisco is much faster and more high-pitched compared to regular Disco. There’s also very little bass, but a constant repetitive rhythm made futuristic by modulations. A specific trademark of Eurodisco was the ‘megamix’ – ultralong edits consisting of different songs mixed together to create a non-stop dancing machine. These mixes showcased producers who became the very first electronic dance DJ’s. Italy developed the biggest and most familiar subgenre of Eurodisco – Italo-Disco, full of silly melodies and lyrics that gave new meaning to the word catchy, whereas France took a more futuristic and instrumental approach with Space Disco.
