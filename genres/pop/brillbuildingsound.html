BRILL BUILDING SOUND


Pop music is a commercial and light variant of either Rock or R&B music, often crossing over to whatever genre sells the most. However, before Rock music, producers were already creating Pop music. The dominant source of Pop in the early 20th century was Tin Pan Alley: a musical neighbourhood in New York near and for Broadway. Nearby was the famous Brill Building. When EP’s became more and more commonplace in the fifties and the first hit parade appeared, this building turned into a giant busy hive, filled with countless recording studios. At first, Brill Building’s focus lay at creating music for the family, but that all changed in the late fifties. Charming male crooners and hired girl groups were meant to express an irresistible image of sexiness, turning their work into large profits. The first crooners appeared in the twenties and were named after their singing technique of gently moaning in the microphone. Brill Building Pop evolved from a more minimal singer/songwriter type of music into a light version of Rock ‘n’ Roll written in 6/8 signature with Latin or exotic sounds, strings, children’s melodies and Rhythm ‘n’ Blues influences.
