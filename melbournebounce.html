MELBOURNE BOUNCE

2012

Melbourne Bounce is a subgenre of Electro House that started in Melbourne, Australia. It is defined by its evolution from the fast-paced, horn-heavy Dutch House style. What distinguishes Melbourne Bounce from other House genres is its characteristic bouncy bassline, energetic beats, and use of percussive elements like horns and vocal samples. Typically set at 128 BPM, though sometimes reaching up to 150 BPM, Melbourne Bounce is known for its bouncy offbeat bass, high-pitched vocal cuts, raucous horns, and 8-bar snare fills leading up to the drop. This genre often features a repetitive beat structure with various build-ups and moderate drops. It began as a fusion of different elements and the underground Melbourne scene. Gaining traction around mid to late 2012, it steadily rose in popularity from 2013 onwards. By 2014, pioneers like Will Sparks, Joel Fletcher, and Deorro played pivotal roles in popularising this genre, often incorporating quirky and playful sounds that add to its distinctiveness. This led to it being played both locally and internationally.
